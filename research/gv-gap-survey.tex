¥section{1) Improving the Generator Using the Validator}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} use verifier/judge/ranker signal to improve generation quality beyond plain supervised finetuning (SFT), either via training objectives or inference-time reranking.

¥textbf{Key papers and relevance.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item ¥textit{Training Verifiers to Solve Math Word Problems} (Cobbe et al., 2021): foundational verifier-reranking evidence.
  ¥item ¥textit{Let窶冱 Verify Step by Step} (Lightman et al., 2023): process-level validator supervision with substantial gains on math reasoning.
  ¥item ¥textit{DPO} (Rafailov et al., 2023), ¥textit{RRHF} (Yuan et al., 2023), ¥textit{ReST} (Gulcehre et al., 2023): converting ranking/preference signal into generator improvements.
  ¥item ¥textit{LLM-Blender} (Jiang et al., 2023): pairwise ranking + fusion for better outputs.
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} Cobbe $¥rightarrow$ Lightman $¥rightarrow$ Uesato (process vs outcome) $¥rightarrow$ DPO/RRHF $¥rightarrow$ LLM-Blender.

¥textbf{Open questions.} When does validator-driven tuning beat stronger SFT data curation? How to prevent reward hacking against a fixed validator?

¥section{2) Applications of Closing the Gap}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} practical systems where generator--validator consistency improves utility and reliability.

¥textbf{Key applications with concrete evidence.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item ¥textbf{LLM-as-judge:} MT-Bench/Arena (Zheng et al., 2023), G-Eval (Liu et al., 2023), PandaLM/Prometheus.
  ¥item ¥textbf{Revision and factuality:} RARR, SelfCheckGPT, Chain-of-Verification.
  ¥item ¥textbf{Ranking and output selection:} RankGen, LLM-Blender.
  ¥item ¥textbf{Alignment/reward pipelines:} DPO, Constitutional AI.
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} MT-Bench $¥rightarrow$ G-Eval/Prometheus $¥rightarrow$ SelfCheckGPT/RARR $¥rightarrow$ RankGen/Blender.

¥textbf{Open questions.} Which applications require strict consistency vs calibrated uncertainty? How much human oversight remains necessary at deployment?

¥section{3) Mechanism: Why Disagreement Happens}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} mismatch between latent knowledge, readout, and prompting interface can produce generation--validation disagreement.

¥textbf{Key papers and relevance.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item ¥textit{Language Models (Mostly) Know What They Know} (Kadavath et al., 2022): latent-knowledge vs expression gap.
  ¥item ¥textit{Discovering Latent Knowledge Without Supervision} (Burns et al., 2022): latent truth signals can exist despite poor surface answers.
  ¥item ¥textit{Unfaithful Explanations in CoT} (Turpin et al., 2023): generated rationales may not track true computation.
  ¥item ¥textit{DoLa}, ¥textit{Tuned Lens}, ¥textit{Representation Engineering}: evidence that layer-level readout choices matter.
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} Kadavath/Burns $¥rightarrow$ Turpin $¥rightarrow$ Tuned Lens/DoLa $¥rightarrow$ RankAlign.

¥textbf{Open questions.} Do alignment methods increase shared generator--validator mechanisms, or only output-level agreement?

¥section{4) Directionality: Generator$¥rightarrow$Validator vs Validator$¥rightarrow$Generator}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} direction choice should depend on data regime and validator quality.

¥textbf{Empirical pattern.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item Validator-first is strong when decomposable checks and process labels are available (Cobbe, Lightman, Uesato).
  ¥item Generator-first is often used in preference-alignment regimes (RLHF/RLAIF/DPO/RRHF), then validator quality is improved iteratively.
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} Cobbe/Lightman/Uesato $¥rightarrow$ RLHF/RLAIF/DPO/RRHF $¥rightarrow$ Self-rewarding models.

¥textbf{Open questions.} Can alternating co-training avoid evaluator gaming and collapse? Which diagnostics should gate direction switches?

¥section{5) Generalization Across Tasks/Domains/Scales}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} test whether gains transfer beyond one benchmark.

¥textbf{Key observations.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item RankAlign and Li et al. report cross-task effects but with remaining domain gaps.
  ¥item Verifier/process methods generalize best within reasoning-heavy domains, less reliably across distant tasks.
  ¥item Reranking/fusion methods transfer partially but depend on candidate diversity and score calibration.
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} RankAlign $¥rightarrow$ Li et al. $¥rightarrow$ verifier/process papers $¥rightarrow$ ranker-fusion papers.

¥textbf{Open questions.} What are robust OOD stress tests for consistency transfer?

¥section{6) Evaluation Design: Artifact vs True Inconsistency}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} measured inconsistency can be inflated by evaluation protocol choices.

¥textbf{Key papers and relevance.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item ¥textit{PromptBench} and ¥textit{Fantastically Ordered Prompts}: prompt-ordering and formatting effects.
  ¥item ¥textit{Lost in the Middle}: context position effects.
  ¥item ¥textit{POSIX}: prompt sensitivity index for controlled robustness measurement.
  ¥item Evaluator-bias papers (G-Eval, fairness/position-bias studies): protocol-induced judge instability.
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} PromptBench/POSIX $¥rightarrow$ Lost in the Middle $¥rightarrow$ judge-bias studies.

¥textbf{Open questions.} Standardized artifact-adjusted metrics for GV gap remain underdeveloped.

¥section{7) Training Targets: Pairwise Ranking vs Process Supervision}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} pairwise preference objectives are useful but often insufficient for hard reasoning without process traces.

¥textbf{Key evidence.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item Pairwise/preference: RLHF, RLAIF, DPO, RRHF.
  ¥item Process/outcome split: Uesato et al.
  ¥item Process-supervised verifiers: Let窶冱 Verify Step by Step, Math-Shepherd.
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} RLHF $¥rightarrow$ DPO/RRHF $¥rightarrow$ Uesato $¥rightarrow$ Lightman/Math-Shepherd.

¥textbf{Open questions.} Cost-effective process labels; hybrid objectives that preserve diversity without sacrificing correctness.

¥section{8) Inference-Time Compute}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} substantial gap reduction can be achieved without retraining via sampling, voting, reranking, and search.

¥textbf{Key papers and relevance.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item ¥textit{Self-Consistency} (Wang et al., 2022): majority over sampled traces.
  ¥item ¥textit{Tree of Thoughts}, ¥textit{LATS}: explicit search/planning at inference time.
  ¥item Verifier reranking (Cobbe, Lightman) and iterative critique (Self-Refine, Reflexion).
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} Self-Consistency $¥rightarrow$ verifier reranking $¥rightarrow$ ToT/LATS $¥rightarrow$ iterative critique.

¥textbf{Open questions.} Compute--quality frontiers, diminishing returns, and robustness under distribution shift.

¥section{9) Judge Reliability and Calibration}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} validators are useful but fragile; reliability is conditional.

¥textbf{Key papers and relevance.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item MT-Bench/Arena, G-Eval, PandaLM, JudgeLM, Prometheus: judge capability baselines.
  ¥item Fairness/position-bias and calibration works (Judging the Judges, Split and Merge, CalibraEval, Juries): identify systematic judge failure modes.
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} MT-Bench/G-Eval $¥rightarrow$ JudgeLM/Prometheus $¥rightarrow$ bias/calibration papers.

¥textbf{Open questions.} Robust calibration standards for judges and trigger conditions for human escalation.

¥section{10) Origin of the Gap and Post-Training Effects}
¥textbf{Core contribution of this line:} gap appears to arise from a combination of pretraining objective/readout mismatch and post-training objective shifts.

¥textbf{Key evidence.}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item Knowledge/calibration line: Kadavath papers, semantic uncertainty, TruthfulQA.
  ¥item Post-training effects: InstructGPT, Constitutional AI, DPO alter response style and confidence behavior.
  ¥item RankAlign/Li et al. directly quantify and reduce generator--validator discrepancy.
¥end{itemize}

¥textbf{Reading roadmap.} Kadavath/TruthfulQA $¥rightarrow$ InstructGPT/Constitutional/DPO $¥rightarrow$ Li et al./RankAlign.

¥textbf{Open questions.} How much of the residual gap is representational vs purely decoding/protocol-induced?

¥section{Cross-Topic Unresolved Questions}
¥begin{itemize}
  ¥item Unified benchmark suite that separates true inconsistency from protocol artifact.
  ¥item Principled objective for jointly training generator and validator without collapse or gaming.
  ¥item Mechanistic overlap metrics to test whether alignment changes internal computation or only outputs.
  ¥item Reliable, calibrated, adversarially robust validator standards for deployment.
¥end{itemize}
